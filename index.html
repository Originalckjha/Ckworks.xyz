The Siddhant

Non-Negotiable Axioms

Teaching as Tapasya

True teaching is not information transfer—it's transformation. Each session demands complete presence, radical patience, and the willingness to explain the same concept fifteen different ways until clarity arrives. This is disciplined practice, not transactional work.

"The purpose of education is not to validate what you know, but to confront what you don't."

Code as Karma

Every commit carries intention. Write for the maintainer who will inherit your decisions at 2 AM. Clean code is selfless code. Technical debt is unresolved karma—it compounds until addressed.

"You have a right to perform your prescribed duties, but you are not entitled to the fruits of your actions." — Bhagavad Gita 2.47

Simplicity as Samadhi

Complexity is ego masquerading as competence. The interface that disappears is the interface that serves. Remove until you cannot remove anymore. What remains is essence.

"Perfection is achieved not when there is nothing more to add, but when there is nothing left to take away." — Antoine de Saint-Exupéry

Students as Stakeholders

Every student is an investment in future possibility. Their success is not my credential—it's their inheritance. Teach them to think, question, and build. Then step aside.

"When the student is ready, the teacher appears. When the student is truly ready, the teacher disappears."

Open Source as Yajna

Knowledge hoarded is knowledge wasted. Share without expectation. Document for strangers. Contribute to the commons. This is the highest form of work.

"The gift given without expectation of return, at the proper time and place, to a worthy recipient, is sattvic." — Bhagavad Gita 17.20
